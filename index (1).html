The Story of NayePankh
"नये पंख" — New Wings

🌱 How the Name Was Born

In Hindi, "Naye Pankh" means New Wings. The name was chosen deliberately — not just as a metaphor for freedom, but as a lived belief. When a young girl receives education, a woman gets access to menstrual hygiene, or a hungry child gets a warm meal — they aren't being helped. They're being given wings they already deserved.

The founders, a group of passionate students from Kanpur, saw something stark around them: the same society that produced engineers and doctors also had families who couldn't afford food or sanitary pads. They decided to act locally, while thinking globally. And they needed a name that wasn't about charity — it was about dignity and possibility. NayePankh was born.

🔥 The Spark: A Real Incident

The trigger wasn't a boardroom decision. It was a conversation with a girl who had missed weeks of school because she had no sanitary pads and couldn't afford them. She wasn't unintelligent. She wasn't unmotivated. She was simply failed by a gap in access. That conversation lit a fire — "if not us, who? If not now, when?"

📍 Early Days

Starting with barely 10 volunteers and very limited funds, the foundation held its first food drive in Kanpur — distributing meals to over 200 families. Word spread. Students from other colleges joined. The foundation grew to cover Ghaziabad and several other UP cities, and has since assisted over 2 lakh people across food, clothing, education, and hygiene drives.

🏛️ Official Recognition

NayePankh Foundation is today registered with the UP Government and holds 80G & 12A certifications under the Indian Income Tax Act — meaning every donation you make is tax-exempt. It is recognized as one of the largest student-led NGOs in India.

Period Awareness

Menstruation is not a taboo

In India, over 300 million women menstruate every month, yet the majority lack access to safe, hygienic products. Girls miss school. Women miss work. And silence keeps the cycle going.

NayePankh Foundation works to break that silence — distributing sanitary pads, conducting awareness workshops in schools and communities, and creating a world where getting your period doesn't cost you your future.

Myth Women shouldn't enter the kitchen or temple during menstruation.
Fact Menstruation is a completely normal biological process. There are no medical, scientific, or valid religious reasons to restrict a menstruating person's activities.
Myth Talking about periods is shameful or inappropriate.
Fact Open conversation about periods saves lives. Girls who understand menstrual health stay in school, seek medical help when needed, and live healthier lives.
The Menstrual Cycle
🌑
Menstrual Phase
Days 1–5. The uterine lining sheds. Cramping is normal — it does not mean weakness.
🌿
Follicular Phase
Days 1–13. Energy rises. The body prepares an egg for possible fertilization.
🌕
Ovulation
Day 14. The egg is released. Peak fertility and energy — this is not a disease.
🍂
Luteal Phase
Days 15–28. The body winds down. PMS symptoms like fatigue are real and valid.
